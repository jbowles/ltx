Eng 2020 Terminology

May 25, 2010

LOGIC AND ARGUMENTATION Argument An activity that employs a reasoning structure in a setting with a task/goal. Belief Conviction that a proposition is justified and true. Claim A proposition that, ideally, has some type of evidence or justification to support it. Context Combination of audience, purpose, and intent; the communicative situation. Evidence Information used to support claims and assertions; e.g., FACTS, STATISTICS, LOGICAL INFERENCES, TESTIMONY, EMPIRICAL DATA. Fallacy False reasoning that appears to be true. Justification Why a belief is true. Knowledge Justified true belief. Logic The reasoning structure of a sequence of statments; there are a number of types of logic: inductive, deductive, and abductive. Proposition A statement that is true or false. Syllogism A logical form of argument in which the conclusion follows directly fom the premises. Warrant The motivation for thinking there is a justification for a belief.

RHETORIC AND COMPOSITION Audience The set of people who will read your text. Appeals to an audience consist of ETHOS, PATHOS, LOGOS. There is an ideal and a real audience. Composition To put together or build from smaller parts. Genre Categories of conventional language-related and rhetorical domains. Rhetoric The technique (or technical skill) of structuring information effectively in order to communicate.
